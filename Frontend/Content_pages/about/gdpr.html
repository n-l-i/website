What data do we collect?

Personal identification information:

  • Email address
  • IP addresses
  • Password

Other data:

  • User created data, such as messages you have sent
  • Preferences data, such as preferred language and colour themes
  • Session data, such as the state of current chess games

How do we collect your data?

You directly provide us with most of the data we collect. We collect data and process data when you:

  • Register an account on our website
    (Email address, password, and preferences data)
  • Edit account settings on our website
    (Preferences data)
  • Submit data to our website
    (User created data)

We may also receive your data indirectly from the following sources:

  • Register an account on our website
    (IP addresses)
  • Sign in to our website
    (IP addresses)
  • Use our website
    (Session data)

How will we use your data?

We collect your data so that we can:

  • Manage your account.
  • Enhance your experience on the website.

We will not share this collected data with any other entities. Some of the user created data we collect might be publicly available but if so it will not be tied to any personal identification information.

When processing your data, the data will be transmitted between your device and our servers.

How do we store your data?

We securely store your data on our servers in the Amazon Web Services Elastic Compute Cloud (AWS EC2). All personal identification data, except for your email address, is thoroughly and securely hashed. This means that the data, in the form that it is stored, carries no meaning beyond identifying you on this specific website. In addition to this, all data is also encrypted to make your data be as secure as possible even in the event of a data breach.

Here is a summary:

  • Email address: Encrypted.
  • IP addresses: Hashed and encrypted.
  • Password: Hashed and encrypted.
  • User created data: Encrypted.
  • Preferences data: Encrypted.
  • Session data: Encrypted.

We will keep your data for a maximum of one year after your last sign in. We will email you to inform you when this is about to happen so that you can prevent it if desired. Once this time period has expired, we will delete your data by removing all instances of your data, including all references to any of your data, from our databases.

What are your data protection rights?

We would like to make sure you are fully aware of all of your data protection rights. Every user is entitled to the following:

  • The right to access – You have the right to request copies of your personal data from us. We may charge you a small fee for this service.
  • The right to rectification – You have the right to request that we correct any information you believe is inaccurate. You also have the right to request us to complete the information you believe is incomplete.
  • The right to erasure – You have the right to request that we erase your personal data, under certain conditions.
  • The right to restrict processing – You have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal data, under certain conditions.
  • The right to object to processing – You have the right to object to our processing of your personal data, under certain conditions.
  • The right to data portability – You have the right to request that we transfer the data that we have collected to another organization, or directly to you, under certain conditions.

If you make a request, we have one month to respond to you. We will try to respond as quickly as possible. If you would like to exercise any of these rights, please contact us at our email: [EMAIL_ADDRESS]

Cookies

Cookies are text files placed on your computer to store various data to be sent between your computer and our servers. This website also uses a different but related technology called localstorage which enables us to store data and process data on your computer without needing to send it to our servers as much. We will refer to all of these technologies as "cookies" for brevity.

For further information, visit allaboutcookies.org.

How do we use cookies?

Our Company uses cookies to improve your experience on our website. We use cookies to:

  • Keep you signed in
  • Keep track of which page you currently have open
  • Keep track of your selected preferences

Our website uses purely functionality cookies. We use them to recognize you on our website and remember your previously selected preferences.

How to manage your cookies

You can set your browser not to accept cookies, and the website linked above tells you how to remove cookies from your browser. However, in a few cases, some of our website features may not function as a result.
